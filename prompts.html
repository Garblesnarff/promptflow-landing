🧭Business Strategy (Prompts 1–5)

Prompt 1
Analyze my business idea: [describe your business]. What are the 3 biggest risks, 3 biggest opportunities, and 3 assumptions I need to validate before investing more? Be direct — I need critical thinking, not cheerleading.
Use this before making any major bet. Forces honest analysis instead of confirmation bias.
Prompt 2
My business does [describe what you do] for [describe your customers]. Write a one-sentence value proposition that a 10-year-old could understand, a version for LinkedIn, and a version for a sales pitch deck.
Most businesses can't articulate what they do in one sentence. This forces clarity that improves every communication.
Prompt 3
I'm deciding between these two strategic options: [Option A] vs [Option B]. My business goals are [goals], my constraints are [constraints], and my risk tolerance is [low/medium/high]. Give me a structured comparison — pros, cons, and your recommendation with reasoning.
Replaces hours of agonizing with structured analysis. Best for pricing, hiring, channel, and pivot decisions.
Prompt 4
Act as a skeptical board member reviewing my plan: [paste your plan or idea]. What are the 5 most likely failure modes? For each, what's the early warning sign I should watch for?
Pre-mortem thinking. Identifies blind spots before they become expensive mistakes.
Prompt 5
I run [type of business] generating [monthly revenue]. My top 3 expenses are [list them]. Identify 3 ways I could realistically increase revenue by 20% in the next 90 days without adding headcount, and 2 ways I could cut costs without hurting quality.
Forces revenue-focused thinking. Works best when you give real numbers.

💰Sales & Revenue (Prompts 6–10)

Prompt 6
Write a cold outreach email for [product/service] targeted at [job title] at [company type]. The email should be under 100 words, lead with a specific pain point they likely have, and end with a low-friction ask (not "schedule a demo" — something smaller).
Short, specific cold emails convert better than long ones. The "low-friction ask" instruction is key.
Prompt 7
My prospect just said "[their objection]." I sell [product/service] at [price point]. Write 3 different responses to this objection — one that reframes the concern, one that uses social proof, and one that asks a clarifying question to understand the real objection.
Having 3 response options lets you choose based on the prospect's personality and relationship stage.
Prompt 8
I want to upsell my [existing customers who bought X] on [Y product/service]. Write a short email (under 150 words) that references their prior purchase, explains why Y is a natural next step, and creates urgency without fake scarcity.
Upsell emails to existing customers have 3-5x higher conversion than cold outreach. The "no fake scarcity" instruction keeps it credible.
Prompt 9
Create a simple referral program structure for [my business type]. Include: the incentive for referrers, the incentive for new customers, how to track referrals simply (no expensive software), and a short script for asking existing customers to refer someone.
Referral programs are the highest-ROI growth channel for service businesses. This gives you a working structure in 2 minutes.
Prompt 10
Review this proposal I'm sending to a client: [paste proposal]. What are the 3 weakest parts that a buyer would use to negotiate down or say no? Rewrite those sections to be stronger. Don't change the price — just improve the justification and framing.
Having AI red-team your proposal before sending it catches the obvious holes clients would exploit.

📣Marketing & Content (Prompts 11–15)

Prompt 11
Write a 30-day content calendar for [my business type] targeting [audience]. Include: 4 educational posts, 4 behind-the-scenes posts, 4 social proof posts, 4 promotional posts, and 2 engagement posts per week. Format as a table with day, content type, topic, and key message.
A full month's content strategy in 60 seconds. Adjust the mix based on your current growth stage.
Prompt 12
I need 10 LinkedIn post hooks for [my industry/role] that are NOT generic. Each hook should name a specific pain point, counterintuitive insight, or surprising fact that my target audience ([describe them]) would stop scrolling for. No "I learned something important" openers.
The hook is 80% of a post's performance. This forces specificity over generic engagement-bait.
Prompt 13
Repurpose this [blog post / email / podcast transcript]: [paste content]. Create: 3 LinkedIn posts, 5 tweets, 1 email newsletter paragraph, and 3 short-form video script hooks. Keep the core insight consistent but adapt the format and length for each platform.
Content repurposing is the highest-leverage marketing activity. One piece of content becomes 12.
Prompt 14
Write a product description for [product/service] using the PAS framework (Problem → Agitate → Solution). Target audience: [describe]. Price: [price]. Make the problem section feel painfully relatable, not generic. End with a specific CTA.
PAS-structured copy consistently outperforms feature-list copy because it leads with the customer's experience, not your product.
Prompt 15
Analyze my competitor [competitor name or describe their positioning]. What is their messaging strategy? What customer pain points do they NOT address? What would make a customer choose me over them — and how should I communicate that differentiation in my marketing?
Competitive positioning research that used to take hours of analysis. Works best when you give the competitor's website or tagline.

👥Hiring & Team (Prompts 16–18)

Prompt 16
Write a job description for [role] at [company type/size]. Include: the 3 outcomes this person is responsible for in their first 90 days, the non-negotiable skills, and the "nice to haves." Avoid corporate jargon. Make it sound like a human wrote it, not HR software.
Outcome-focused job descriptions attract better candidates and set clear expectations from day one.
Prompt 17
I'm interviewing a candidate for [role]. Their background is [summary]. Write 5 behavioral interview questions that test for [specific skill or trait]. Include what a strong answer looks like and what a weak answer looks like for each question.
Knowing what good and bad answers sound like lets you evaluate candidates consistently, not just by gut feel.
Prompt 18
I need to give difficult feedback to a team member about [specific behavior or performance issue]. They are [describe their personality/sensitivity level]. Write a script for the conversation using the SBI model (Situation → Behavior → Impact). Keep it direct but not harsh.
SBI feedback is specific and actionable. The script format reduces avoidance — you know exactly what to say.

📊Finance & Operations (Prompts 19–22)

Prompt 19
Help me build a simple financial forecast for the next 12 months. My current monthly revenue is [amount], cost of goods is [%], fixed costs are [amount], and variable costs are [describe]. Create a table showing revenue, costs, and net profit under 3 scenarios: conservative (−20%), base, and optimistic (+30%).
Scenario planning prevents both overconfidence and panic. The 3-scenario format forces you to think about what could go wrong AND right.
Prompt 20
I'm considering a price increase from [current price] to [new price] for [product/service]. My current customer retention rate is [%] and I have [number] of customers. Model the financial impact: how many customers can I afford to lose and still break even? What's the net revenue difference at 5%, 10%, and 20% churn?
Most business owners are afraid to raise prices because they imagine losing all their customers. This math usually shows the fear is overblown.
Prompt 21
Review this vendor contract section: [paste section]. Identify any clauses that are unusually risky for me as the buyer, any auto-renewal terms, any liability caps I should negotiate, and anything that's non-standard. Explain each issue in plain English.
Not a substitute for a lawyer on major contracts, but invaluable for flagging issues before you pay for legal review.
Prompt 22
I spend [X hours] per week on [describe task]. Help me decide whether to automate, delegate, or eliminate it. Consider: what's my effective hourly rate ([annual revenue / 2080 hours]), what would it cost to automate or delegate this, and what's the ROI over 12 months? Give me a clear recommendation.
Forces explicit ROI thinking before you commit to a tool, hire, or process change.

⚡AI Productivity (Prompts 23–25)

Prompt 23
I have these tasks to complete today: [paste your task list]. Given that my most important goal this week is [goal], reorder this list by impact × urgency. Flag any tasks I should delegate or drop entirely. Give me a realistic estimate of how long the remaining tasks will take.
AI as a ruthless prioritization partner. Works best first thing in the morning with your actual to-do list.
Prompt 24
Summarize this email thread and tell me: (1) what decisions have been made, (2) what's still unresolved, (3) what I need to respond to, and (4) the most important thing to say in my reply. [Paste email thread]
The highest-ROI prompt for anyone who deals with long email threads. Turns 15 minutes of reading into 30 seconds.
Prompt 25
I need to write a [type of document: report / proposal / memo / SOPs] about [topic]. My audience is [describe]. The key points I need to convey are: [list 3-5 bullet points]. Write a complete first draft, then tell me the 2 weakest sections that need improvement.
Self-critique built into the prompt. Forces the AI to flag its own weaknesses rather than presenting everything as polished.
