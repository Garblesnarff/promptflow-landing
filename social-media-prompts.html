25 Free ChatGPT Prompts for Social Media (2026)

Social media content doesn't have to take 3 hours a day. These 25 prompts cover every major platform — Instagram captions, TikTok scripts, LinkedIn posts, Twitter threads, and content planning. Replace the [BRACKETS], paste into any AI tool, and post in minutes.

📸 Instagram (Prompts 1–6)

Instagram
Prompt 1
Caption that stops the scroll
Write an Instagram caption for a [NICHE] account posting about [TOPIC]. The caption must: open with a single-line hook that creates curiosity or speaks directly to [AUDIENCE'S PAIN POINT], deliver real value in 3-4 short sentences, end with an engagement question that's easy to answer. Include 15 relevant hashtags. Tone: [YOUR BRAND VOICE]. Keep paragraphs to 1-2 lines max.
Why it works: The opening line is the only thing most followers read before deciding to tap "more." One line, one hook, high stakes.
Instagram
Prompt 2
Hot take that drives comments
Write a "hot take" Instagram caption on [TOPIC IN YOUR NICHE]. State an opinion that 30% of your audience might disagree with — but that you genuinely believe and can defend. Back it up in 3 sentences of reasoning. End with a direct question inviting responses. Be bold but not offensive. Goal: spark a real conversation, not just likes.
Why it works: Controversial opinions drive 3-5x more comments than safe content. Engagement = reach.
Instagram
Prompt 3
5 opening hooks for any post
Write 5 Instagram caption hooks (first line only) for a post about [TOPIC]. Each must be compelling enough to make someone tap "more." Use these 5 formulas: 1. A question they're already asking themselves 2. A bold, specific claim 3. A relatable situation ("When you...") 4. A surprising fact or statistic 5. "If you [X] you need to read this" Rate each 1-10 for likely engagement with [AUDIENCE].
Why it works: Top creators test multiple hooks before posting. This generates 5 options in 30 seconds.
Instagram
Prompt 4
Product launch without leading with the product
Write an Instagram caption for the launch of [PRODUCT/SERVICE] in [NICHE]. Rule: DO NOT lead with the product. Lead with the problem it solves or the transformation it enables. Build desire through the post — only reveal the product/service in the last 20%. End with CTA: "Link in bio" or "DM me [WORD] for details." Under 250 words.
Why it works: Captions that open with a product get skipped. Captions that open with a problem get read.
Instagram
Prompt 5
Carousel script (slide by slide)
Write a 7-slide Instagram carousel about [TOPIC] for [AUDIENCE]. Structure: Slide 1: Hook headline (creates curiosity, promises outcome) Slides 2-6: One insight or step per slide (short, punchy — under 20 words each) Slide 7: CTA (save this, follow for more, or direct offer) Cover slide text: [TOPIC IN 5 WORDS MAX]. Each slide should work as a standalone sentence — readable in a glance.
Why it works: Carousels generate 3x more saves than single-image posts. Saves = algorithmic reach.
Instagram
Prompt 6
Reel script (15-30 seconds)
Write a 15-30 second Reel script for [TOPIC] in [NICHE]. Format: Hook (0-3 sec): Say something that makes someone stop scrolling — a bold statement or relatable situation Value (3-20 sec): Deliver 3 quick tips or a mini-tutorial, one per beat CTA (20-30 sec): Tell them exactly what to do next (follow, save, comment X) Add: B-roll suggestions for each section. Tone: [conversational/authoritative/entertaining].
Why it works: Reels are the highest-reach format on Instagram in 2026. A 3-second hook determines whether the algorithm shows the rest.

🎵 TikTok & Short-Form Video (Prompts 7–11)

TikTok
Prompt 7
TikTok hook (the first 3 seconds)
Write 5 opening lines for a TikTok video about [TOPIC]. Each must make a viewer stop scrolling in the first 3 seconds. Use these hook types: 1. Pattern interrupt ("Nobody talks about this...") 2. Callout ("If you're a [SPECIFIC PERSON], watch this") 3. Bold claim ("I [achieved result] in [time frame]") 4. Curiosity gap ("The reason most people fail at [X] isn't what you think") 5. Relatable frustration ("Why does [common problem] always happen?") Rate each for TikTok virality potential.
Why it works: TikTok's algorithm makes a watch-time decision within 3 seconds. The hook is 80% of the outcome.
TikTok
Prompt 8
Full TikTok script (60 seconds)
Write a 60-second TikTok script for [TOPIC] targeting [AUDIENCE]. Structure: 0-3s: Hook — stop the scroll 3-10s: Promise — what they'll learn in this video 10-50s: Value delivery — 3 specific tips or steps, each 10-15 seconds 50-60s: CTA — comment, follow, or click link Requirements: conversational tone (sounds like talking, not reading), each tip must be specific and actionable. Add notes about gestures or visual suggestions. Goal: maximum watch-through rate.
Why it works: Most TikTok scripts ramble. This structure keeps every second earning its place.
TikTok
Prompt 9
TikTok text overlay script
Write a "talking head + text overlay" TikTok script about [TOPIC]. Format: 3-column table: Column 1: What I SAY (spoken words) Column 2: What appears as TEXT OVERLAY (shorter, reinforcing or contrasting the speech) Column 3: Timing (seconds) Make the text overlays work as standalone content for people watching on mute (30% of TikTok viewers watch without sound). Duration: 45-60 seconds.
Why it works: Text overlays double your reach — viewers who mute still get value, and the visual contrast keeps viewers engaged.
TikTok
Prompt 10
TikTok stitch or duet response
Write a TikTok stitch script responding to the following claim: "[PASTE THE CLAIM OR QUOTE FROM ANOTHER VIDEO]". My angle: [agree/disagree/add nuance]. Structure: 0-5s: React to the clip (one sentence). 5-40s: Build my case — 3 specific counterpoints or additions with examples. 40-50s: Summary of my actual position. 50-60s: question that invites comments. Make it add genuine value to the conversation, not just clout-chasing.
Why it works: Stitch and duet content gets surfaced to the original video's audience — instant reach on a warm audience.
TikTok
Prompt 11
TikTok Series concept
Create a 5-video TikTok series concept on [BROAD TOPIC] for [AUDIENCE]. For each video: title/hook (the first line), core content (2-3 sentences), cliffhanger or reason to watch the next video, and how this connects to [MY PRODUCT/SERVICE OR CTA]. Make each video valuable standalone but even better as part of the series. Include the series hashtag structure.
Why it works: Series content drives follows — viewers who want to see part 2 follow to be notified. Follows compound into long-term reach.

💼 LinkedIn (Prompts 12–16)

LinkedIn
Prompt 12
LinkedIn post that gets saved and shared
Write a LinkedIn post about [TOPIC] for [TARGET PROFESSIONAL AUDIENCE]. Structure: Line 1: Single bold statement or counterintuitive claim (no "I'm excited to share...") Lines 2-3: Why this matters or a brief personal hook Lines 4-10: The value — 4-6 actionable insights, short paragraphs, white space between each Final line: Open-ended question that invites peer-level discussion Goal: saves and shares, not just likes. Avoid: corporate buzzwords, excessive humility, oversharing personal life.
Why it works: LinkedIn rewards dwell time and saves. Scannable structure with real insight gets both.
LinkedIn
Prompt 13
Story post about a professional lesson
Write a LinkedIn story post about [A PROFESSIONAL EXPERIENCE OR LESSON — e.g., "a project that failed", "a client who fired me", "a mistake I made in year 1"]. Structure: specific scene-setting (what happened, when, what was at stake), what I got wrong or what surprised me, what I learned and how I changed because of it, what this means for [AUDIENCE]. Tone: honest, specific, zero corporate speak. The ending should make someone tag a colleague or save the post. Under 300 words.
Why it works: Vulnerability and specificity outperform advice on LinkedIn. "Here's what I got wrong" gets more shares than "Here's how to succeed."
LinkedIn
Prompt 14
LinkedIn carousel text
Write a 10-slide LinkedIn carousel on [TOPIC] for [PROFESSIONAL AUDIENCE]. Each slide: headline (under 10 words), body text (under 50 words), and one visual suggestion. Slide 1: Hook — bold claim or stat Slides 2-9: One insight per slide, building toward a framework or conclusion Slide 10: Summary + CTA (follow for more / download resource / comment question) Make each slide independently shareable — someone should be able to screenshot any slide and post it alone.
Why it works: LinkedIn carousels get 3-5x more impressions than single posts. Each slide is a shareable unit of value.
LinkedIn
Prompt 15
Comment reply that builds your brand
Write a substantive reply to this LinkedIn comment on my post about [TOPIC]: "[PASTE THE COMMENT]". The reply should: add a new insight or angle the commenter didn't mention, acknowledge their point without just agreeing, and end with a question that keeps the thread going. Under 100 words. Tone: [peer-to-peer professional / expert-to-practitioner]. Goal: the comment thread itself becomes content.
Why it works: LinkedIn's algorithm treats long comment threads as high-quality content. Each reply extends your post's reach.
LinkedIn
Prompt 16
Connect without being salesy
Write a LinkedIn connection request note to [TYPE OF PERSON — e.g., "a potential client who commented on my post", "a peer in my industry I want to collaborate with", "a speaker at an event I attended"]. Context: [HOW I KNOW OF THEM]. Requirements: under 300 characters (LinkedIn limit), mentions one specific thing about their work or perspective, no pitch, ends with either a genuine question or just warmth. No "I came across your profile and was impressed by..."
Why it works: Personalized notes get 5x higher acceptance rates. One specific detail signals you actually paid attention.

🐦 Twitter / X (Prompts 17–20)

Twitter / X
Prompt 17
Twitter thread that goes viral
Write a 10-tweet thread about [TOPIC] for [AUDIENCE]. Tweet 1 (hook): bold claim or promise — must make someone click "show more." Tweets 2-9: one insight per tweet, building toward a conclusion. Each tweet: under 280 characters, valuable standalone, ends with a natural transition to the next. Tweet 10: summary + follow CTA. Use numbered format (1/, 2/) and include one question tweet in the middle to drive replies. No filler tweets.
Why it works: Threads get 10x more profile visits than single tweets. The hook tweet determines if anyone reads the rest.
Twitter / X
Prompt 18
Evergreen tweet that gets retweeted for months
Write 5 standalone tweets about [TOPIC] designed to get saves and retweets over time (not just on the day posted). Each tweet: under 240 characters, contains one transferable insight or memorable framing, works without context, doesn't start with "I". Types to try: a counterintuitive truth, a framework in one sentence, a "most people X but actually Y" observation, a specific piece of advice with a number, a question that makes someone think for 30 seconds.
Why it works: Evergreen tweets keep generating impressions for months. They're also the easiest content to repurpose across platforms.
Twitter / X
Prompt 19
Reply that builds your brand without pitching
Write 3 reply options to this tweet: "[PASTE THE TWEET]". Each reply should: add genuine value or a new angle, be under 150 characters, make the original poster likely to like or retweet it. Reply styles: (1) add a related insight, (2) a specific example that supports or nuances the point, (3) a respectful disagreement with reasoning. Do NOT pitch anything. Goal: the reply itself builds your credibility with the original poster's audience.
Why it works: Twitter replies on high-impression accounts expose you to audiences 10x your size. One good reply compounds into followers.
Twitter / X
Prompt 20
Build-in-public thread
Write a #buildinpublic Twitter thread documenting [WHAT I'M BUILDING — project, product, business milestone]. Format: Tweet 1: the outcome or current status (lead with the result). Tweets 2-7: behind-the-scenes: what worked, what failed, a specific number or data point, what I'd do differently. Tweet 8: what's next + what I need help with. Tweet 9: ask for the audience's experience with [TOPIC]. Make it honest, specific, and useful to someone building something similar.
Why it works: #buildinpublic is one of Twitter's highest-engagement formats because it combines transparency, learning, and progress — all of which Twitter users reward.

🗓️ Content Strategy & Planning (Prompts 21–25)

Strategy
Prompt 21
Define your content pillars
I'm a [YOUR ROLE] who creates content for [YOUR AUDIENCE]. Based on my expertise and audience interests, suggest 5 content pillars that would: build my authority, attract ideal followers/clients, and keep me consistently posting without running out of ideas. For each pillar: name it, explain why it resonates with my audience, and give 3 specific post ideas. Then suggest which platform each pillar performs best on and why.
Why it works: Content pillars are the architecture that makes consistent posting feel effortless instead of starting from zero every day.
Strategy
Prompt 22
30-day content calendar
Build a 30-day social media content calendar for [PLATFORM] based on these content pillars: [LIST YOUR 3-5 PILLARS]. Output: a table with Day | Pillar | Post Type (educational/personal/promotional) | Topic/Angle | CTA. Content mix: 60% educational, 25% personal/behind-the-scenes, 15% promotional. Cluster 2-3 posts around [PRODUCT/SERVICE LAUNCH DATE or KEY DATE] without being obviously sales-focused the entire week.
Why it works: A calendar removes the daily "what should I post?" decision that kills consistency.
Strategy
Prompt 23
Repurpose one post into 5 formats
I posted this on [PLATFORM]: [PASTE YOUR POST]. Repurpose it into 5 platform-specific formats: 1. Instagram caption (under 250 words, with hook + hashtags) 2. LinkedIn post (professional angle, no hashtags) 3. Twitter thread (5-7 tweets) 4. TikTok script (30-second hook + value) 5. Short-form email newsletter intro (under 200 words) Keep the core insight but adapt the format, length, and tone for each platform's culture.
Why it works: One idea → 5 posts. The content creation time stays constant while the output multiplies 5x.
Strategy
Prompt 24
Audit your social media performance
Here's my social media data from the last 30 days: [PASTE: platform, top posts by reach/engagement, average reach, follower change, best posting time]. Analyze: which content types are outperforming? What pattern explains my top 3 posts? Which content is getting engagement but no reach (good quality, bad distribution)? Which is getting reach but no engagement (bad quality, good timing)? Give me 3 specific changes to make next month based on this data.
Why it works: Most creators post by feel. This prompt forces a data-first review that reveals the actual patterns behind what's working.
Strategy
Prompt 25
Write your social media bio for any platform
Write a social media bio for [PLATFORM] for a [YOUR ROLE/NICHE] who helps [AUDIENCE] achieve [OUTCOME]. Requirements: under [CHARACTER LIMIT — Instagram: 150, Twitter: 160, LinkedIn: 220, TikTok: 80], leads with what I do for others (not my credentials), includes one specific result or credibility signal, ends with a CTA or next step. Write 3 versions: professional, conversational, and results-focused. I'll pick one.
Why it works: Your bio is the first thing a potential follower reads. Most bios describe the person, not the value they get from following.
Pro tip: Combine prompts. Use Prompt 21 (content pillars) first, then Prompt 22 (30-day calendar), then write each day's post using the platform-specific prompts. The full workflow takes about 2 hours and gives you a month of content.
