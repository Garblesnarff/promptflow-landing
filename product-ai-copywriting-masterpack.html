The Frameworks Inside

Framework 1
AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action
The oldest and most proven copywriting structure. Used in every major ad channel since 1898. These 10 prompts force ChatGPT to follow AIDA's logic — hook the reader's eye first, build genuine interest, convert it to desire, then demand action.
Framework 2
PAS — Problem, Agitate, Solution
The framework that made Dan Kennedy famous. Identify the real pain, make it feel urgent, then position your product as relief. PAS-structured prompts write copy that feels like you've read the reader's mind.
Framework 3
FAB — Features, Advantages, Benefits
Most copy fails because it lists features. FAB forces each feature to be translated to its advantage, then to its real customer benefit. These prompts wire that translation into every description you generate.
Framework 4
BAB — Before, After, Bridge
The storytelling structure that sells. Paint where the customer is now (before), where they want to be (after), and show how your product is the bridge. Particularly powerful for email subject lines and social ads.
Framework 5
4Ps — Picture, Promise, Proof, Push
Henry Hoke Sr.'s four-part formula for direct response. Excellent for landing pages and long-form ads where you need to earn trust before the ask.

3 Sample Prompts

Real prompts from the pack. Replace anything in [BRACKETS] with your specifics.

AIDA — Headline Generator
Write 5 headlines for [product/service] using the AIDA framework's first two steps only: grab attention and create interest. Each headline must: (1) call out a specific person or problem, (2) hint at a specific outcome without revealing it, (3) be under 12 words. Product: [describe product]. Target buyer: [describe who they are and what they want].
PAS — Facebook Ad Copy
Write a Facebook ad using PAS for [product]. Problem section: describe the specific frustration [target customer] faces with [the status quo]. 2-3 sentences, make them feel it. Agitation section: add 1-2 consequences they're already experiencing because of this problem. Solution section: introduce [product] as relief — not hype, just clarity on what it does and how to get it. Include a CTA. Total: 150 words max.
FAB — E-Commerce Product Description
Write a product description using the FAB framework for: [product name]. Features: [list 3 key features]. For each feature, write: (1) the feature itself in plain language, (2) its functional advantage, (3) the real customer benefit (emotional or practical). Format as 3 mini-sections. End with one sentence that ties all three benefits into a single value statement. Tone: [casual/professional/playful].
