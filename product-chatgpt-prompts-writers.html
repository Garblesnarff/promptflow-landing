3 Sample Prompts

From the full pack. Replace anything in [BRACKETS] with your specifics.

Blog Writing · Introductions
Write the introduction paragraph (150-200 words) for a blog post titled "[TITLE]". Open with a surprising statistic or specific relatable scenario — not a generic observation. Establish the reader's problem in 2-3 sentences, promise what they'll learn, and naturally include the keyword "[PRIMARY KEYWORD]" within the first 100 words. Do NOT start with "In today's...", "Are you looking for...", or "Have you ever..."
Fiction · Plot Structure
I'm stuck in my story. Here's where I am: [DESCRIBE THE SITUATION]. My protagonist needs to get from [POINT A] to [POINT B] but every path feels contrived. Give me 5 different ways to get them there — each using a different technique: coincidence (then show how to make it not feel like coincidence), character decision driven by flaw, external force, reversal of what the character thinks they want, and escalating consequence of an earlier choice. I'll pick one.
Editing · Clarity
Tighten this passage by 30% without losing any meaning or changing my voice: [PASTE YOUR TEXT]. Cut every word that doesn't carry weight. Eliminate adverbs (replace with stronger verbs). Cut hedging words ("somewhat", "quite", "rather", "a bit"). Remove setup phrases ("I think that...", "It's important to note that..."). Shorten prepositional chains. Show me the before/after word count and flag the 3 most common patterns you cut.
