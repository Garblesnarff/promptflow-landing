5 Sample Prompts

Real prompts from the pack. Replace anything in [BRACKETS] with your specifics before using.

Job Description
Write a compelling job description for a [JOB TITLE] role at a [COMPANY TYPE]. Company description: [1-2 SENTENCES]. The role will be responsible for: [CORE RESPONSIBILITIES]. Required experience: [YEARS/SKILLS]. Tone: [e.g., mission-driven / startup-y / professional]. Include: a strong opening hook (why this role matters), responsibilities (bulleted), qualifications (required vs. preferred clearly separated), compensation range [$X-$Y], benefits summary, and an EOE statement. Avoid jargon and bias-prone language.
LinkedIn Sourcing
Write a LinkedIn InMail for sourcing a [ROLE] candidate who fits this profile: [DESCRIBE IDEAL CANDIDATE PROFILE: skills, experience, industry]. The message should be: under 100 words, specific to something on their profile, explain why the role might interest THEM (not why we need someone), and end with a low-pressure question (not "are you interested?"). Non-generic. Should not sound like a template.
Performance Review
Write a performance review for a [SENIORITY LEVEL] employee in a [ROLE] who: [DESCRIBE THEIR PERFORMANCE — strengths, specific results, development areas]. The review should: acknowledge strengths with evidence, name the development area without blame, connect observations to business impact, and suggest one specific next step. Tone: fair, constructive, specific. Not vague praise or generic criticism. Under 400 words.
Employee Relations
Write a difficult conversation script for a manager who needs to address a conflict between two team members who [DESCRIBE CONFLICT BRIEFLY]. The conversation should: hear both sides separately first, focus on behavior and impact (not character), help both parties identify what they need from each other, and arrive at an agreed working protocol. Include: the separate conversation script and the joint conversation script.
HR Strategy
Write an HR annual strategy presentation outline for a leadership team (30-45 minutes). Sections: year in review (hiring, retention, key metrics), people strategy for next year (priorities, rationale), talent initiatives (L&D, succession planning, diversity), total rewards updates, HR capacity and investment, and key asks from leadership. Include a one-page executive summary format.
